Life-long Learning

The details

I'm always exploring, so, naturally, I'm always learning. Furthermore I'm incapable of being satisfied with knowing anything technological at a superficial level; unavoidably, I always research a technology thoroughly.

I don't do this because of an underlying OCD or anything else medically diagnosable. Rather, I find it very difficult to comprehend any sort of "big picture" without understanding how underlying components interact to form that picture. Knowing the purpose of the components, and how they function in context gives me some level of comfort when I try to build applications with the tools/frameworks or other that they are part of.

Breadth over depth... for now
